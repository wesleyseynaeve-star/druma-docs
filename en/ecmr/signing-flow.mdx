An eCMR requires three signatures to be legally complete: the sender signs at pickup, the driver/carrier signs at pickup, and the consignee signs at delivery. The entire process happens on the driver's phone. Here is how each step works in practice.

---

## Overview of the Three-Party Signing Process

```
Pickup location                    Delivery location
─────────────────────────────      ──────────────────────────
1. Sender signs       →            3. Consignee signs
2. Driver signs       →               ↓
                                    Certified PDF issued
```

Steps 1 and 2 happen at the same time and place (pickup). Step 3 happens later at the delivery location. All three must be completed before TransFollow issues the certified PDF.

---

## Step 1 — Sender Signs at Pickup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Driver opens the eCMR">
    At the pickup location, the driver opens the order in the Druma app and taps the **eCMR** section. The document is displayed showing all pre-filled fields: goods, addresses, truck, driver.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Driver hands the phone to the sender">
    The sender representative (warehouse manager, loading supervisor, or shipper) takes the driver's phone.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sender reviews the document">
    The sender scrolls through the eCMR fields and confirms the goods description, quantity, and condition match what is being loaded.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sender signs with their finger">
    The sender taps **Sign as Sender**, draws their signature on the signature pad, enters their full name and role (e.g., "Warehouse Manager"), and taps **Confirm**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Step 2 — Driver/Carrier Signs at Pickup

Immediately after the sender signs, the driver takes back the phone.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Driver signs">
    The app automatically advances to the carrier signature screen. The driver draws their signature, confirms their name, and taps **Confirm**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pickup signatures locked">
    Both pickup signatures are now transmitted to TransFollow and locked. Neither can be modified. The eCMR status changes to "Awaiting consignee signature."
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The driver must sign immediately after the sender. The app will not allow the driver to proceed to the delivery step until both pickup signatures are complete.
</Note>

---

## Step 3 — Consignee Signs at Delivery

<Steps>
  <Step title="Driver arrives at delivery">
    At the delivery location, the driver opens the order in the Druma app and taps the **eCMR** section. The document now shows the two locked pickup signatures.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Driver hands phone to consignee">
    The consignee representative takes the driver's phone.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Consignee reviews and signs">
    The consignee reviews the document — paying attention to goods condition, any discrepancies, and delivery address — then taps **Sign as Consignee**, draws their signature, enters their name and role, and taps **Confirm**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Certified PDF issued">
    All three signatures are complete. TransFollow immediately generates the certified eCMR PDF. The driver receives a confirmation on screen. The certified PDF is accessible from the order detail page in Druma and can be sent to the client.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## QR Code Option: Consignee Signs on Their Own Device

If the consignee prefers not to sign on the driver's phone — for hygiene reasons, company policy, or simply preference — they can sign on their own device.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap QR Code">
    On the consignee signature screen, the driver taps **Show QR Code**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Consignee scans">
    The consignee scans the QR code with their own phone's camera. This opens a secure, one-time-use web page with the eCMR document.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Consignee signs on their own phone">
    The consignee reviews, signs, enters their name, and confirms. The signature is transmitted directly to TransFollow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Driver sees confirmation">
    The driver's app refreshes and shows the completed signature. The certified PDF is issued.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The QR code link expires after 30 minutes. If the consignee does not sign within that time, the driver can generate a new QR code.
</Note>

---

## Tracking Signature Status

At any time — before, during, or after the signing process — you can check which parties have signed:

1. Open the order in Druma (web or app)
2. Click or tap the **eCMR** tab
3. The status panel shows three rows — Sender, Carrier, Consignee — each showing "Signed ✓" with a timestamp, or "Pending"

Dispatchers in the office can monitor signature status in real time without calling the driver.
