## Overview

The Planning Board is your main dispatch tool. It shows every truck in your fleet on a horizontal timeline, with orders displayed as coloured blocks. You can see what every driver is doing, when their current job finishes, and where the gaps are — all without opening a single order form.

Go to **Planner → Planning Board** to open it.

<Warning>
The Planning Board is designed for desktop screens of 1280 pixels wide or more. On a laptop or a large monitor you get the full experience including drag-and-drop. On a phone or tablet, the board is available in read-only mode — you can view orders but cannot drag or reassign them.
</Warning>

## Reading the board

### The timeline

The horizontal axis is time. Each row represents one truck in your fleet. Orders appear as rectangular blocks spanning from their pickup time to their estimated delivery time.

### Colour coding

The colour of each block tells you the current status of that order at a glance:

| Colour | Status |
|---|---|
| Grey | Draft — not yet confirmed |
| Blue | Confirmed — driver notified, not yet departed |
| Green | In Transit or En Route to Delivery |
| Yellow | Arrived at pickup or delivery — waiting |
| Red | Overdue — past expected delivery time and not yet Delivered |

Keep an eye on red blocks — these need your attention.

### The unassigned sidebar

On the left side of the board, there is a **Unassigned Orders** panel listing all orders that don't have a truck assigned yet. These are the jobs waiting to be dispatched. Drag one from the sidebar onto a truck row to assign it.

## Setting the date range

By default, the board shows today plus the next 7 days. You can change this:

- Click the **date range selector** at the top of the board
- Choose a preset: Today, Next 3 days, This week, Next 2 weeks
- Or pick a custom start and end date

**Keyboard shortcuts for date navigation:**

| Key | Action |
|---|---|
| `←` Left arrow | Move the view one day earlier |
| `→` Right arrow | Move the view one day forward |
| `T` | Jump to today |
| `W` | Switch to week view |
| `M` | Switch to month overview |

## Filtering the board

If you have many trucks, the board can get busy. Use the filters at the top to narrow down what you see:

- **Truck type** — Show only vans, only semis, only flatbeds, etc.
- **Driver** — Show only trucks assigned to a specific driver.
- **Status** — Show only orders in a particular status (e.g., show only Confirmed orders that haven't departed yet).

Filters combine — you can filter by truck type and status at the same time.

## Assigning and reassigning orders

### Assigning from the sidebar

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the unassigned order">
    Locate the order in the Unassigned Orders sidebar on the left. Each card shows the pickup date, origin, destination, and client.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Drag it onto a truck row">
    Click and hold the order card, then drag it onto the row of the truck you want to assign it to. Drop it on the correct date slot.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the assignment">
    A confirmation popup appears showing the truck, driver, and order details. Click **Assign** to confirm. The driver receives a notification automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Reassigning an existing order

To move an order from one truck to another, drag the order block to a different truck row. The same confirmation popup will appear.

<Note>
Druma will warn you if the reassignment creates a time conflict for either the original truck or the new one. You don't have to cancel a reassignment just because of a warning — use your judgement — but the conflict will be highlighted in red so you don't miss it.
</Note>

## Conflicts

If two orders overlap on the same truck — for example, one order's estimated delivery time overlaps with the next order's pickup time — the board highlights the affected blocks in red with a conflict icon.

Conflicts are common when deliveries run late. You have a few options:

- **Drag and reassign** one of the conflicting orders to another truck.
- **Edit the later order's pickup time** to give more slack.
- **Accept the conflict** if you know the driver can make it (e.g., the delivery is actually ahead of schedule).

The conflict indicator does not prevent the order from being dispatched — it's a warning, not a block.

## Viewing order details

**Click** any order block on the board to open a **quick summary panel** on the right side of the screen. This panel shows:

- Pickup and delivery addresses
- Cargo description and weight
- Agreed price
- Current status
- Driver name and phone number

From the quick summary panel you can:

- Click **Edit Order** to open the full order form
- Click **Change Status** to override the status
- Click **Call Driver** to dial the driver's number (opens your phone app)

**Hover** over any block (without clicking) to see a compact tooltip with pickup, delivery, and price — useful when you just want a quick check without opening the full panel.

## Exporting the planning board

You can export the current board view as a PDF — useful for printing a daily dispatch sheet or sharing with a colleague who doesn't have a Druma account.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set your filters and date range">
    Arrange the board to show exactly what you want to export.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Export">
    Find the **Export as PDF** button in the top-right corner of the board.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the layout">
    Select portrait or landscape. Landscape works better for multi-day views.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download">
    Click **Generate PDF**. The file downloads automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The exported PDF is a snapshot of the board at the moment of export. It does not update automatically.
