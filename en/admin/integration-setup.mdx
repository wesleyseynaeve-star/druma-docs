Every integration your company uses is configured in one place: **Settings → Integrations**. You do not need a developer — all setups are form-based and take less than five minutes each.


---

## 1. eCMR Provider

Druma issues electronic consignment notes **in-house using the Druma native provider by default** — no external account, API key, or setup is required. eCMR is available out of the box for cross-border orders, and Druma seals each completed document with a PAdES digital signature.

**TransFollow** remains available as an optional fallback for companies that were already using it before the native provider was introduced. New companies should stay on the native provider.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the eCMR provider card">
    Go to **Settings → Integrations** and open the **eCMR provider** card. The Druma native provider is selected by default.
  </Step>
  <Step title="(Optional) Switch to TransFollow">
    To use TransFollow instead, select it and paste the API key from your TransFollow account dashboard into the **API Key** field. Register at [transfollow.org](https://transfollow.org) if you do not have an account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable mock mode (optional)">
    When using TransFollow, toggle **Mock Mode** on to test the eCMR workflow without issuing real, legally registered documents. Use this during driver training or onboarding. Turn it off before going live.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test the connection">
    Click **Test Connection**. Druma verifies the native signing certificate — or, if TransFollow is selected, calls the TransFollow API to confirm your key is valid.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. eCMR creation is active for cross-border orders.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Most companies never need to change this — the native provider is the recommended default. See [eCMR Providers](/en/ecmr/ecmr-providers) for a full comparison.
</Note>

<Warning>
TransFollow mock-mode eCMR documents are **not legally valid**. Make sure mock mode is off before dispatching real shipments.
</Warning>

## 2. HERE Maps

HERE Maps powers two features in Druma: address autocomplete when creating orders, and truck-optimised route calculation (distance, estimated drive time, and CO2 figures).

**No setup required.** The HERE Maps API key is managed at the platform level by Druma. There is nothing to configure, and you will not be billed separately for HERE usage — it is included in your subscription.

<Note>
If address autocomplete stops working, this is a platform-level issue. Contact Druma support rather than trying to reconfigure HERE yourself.
</Note>

## 3. Fuel Card Import

Druma does not connect directly to fuel card provider APIs. Instead, you download a CSV export from your provider's web portal and upload it into Druma. This keeps fuel costs linked to vehicles and orders automatically.

**Supported providers:** DKV, UTA, AS24, Shell Card, SmartDiesel.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Export from your provider portal">
    Log in to your DKV, UTA, AS24, Shell, or SmartDiesel web portal and export transactions as CSV. Most portals offer this under **Reports → Transaction Export**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Fuel Card Import card">
    In **Settings → Integrations**, click the Fuel Card Import card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your provider">
    Choose your provider from the dropdown so Druma knows which CSV column format to expect.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload the CSV">
    Click **Upload CSV**, select your file, and click **Import**. Druma will match transactions to vehicles by plate number.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
If a transaction's plate number does not match a vehicle in your fleet, Druma will list it as **unmatched**. You can manually assign it or leave it. Regularly check the unmatched list — it often catches fuel card fraud or incorrect plate entries.
</Note>

## 4. SAGA / WinMENTOR Accounting

Druma exports invoice and order data in formats compatible with SAGA and WinMENTOR, the two most common Romanian accounting packages.

### SAGA Classic and WinMENTOR Classic

No API key is needed. Druma generates a structured export file (XML or CSV depending on the package) that you import directly into your accounting software.

Go to **Reports → Accounting Export**, select the date range and accounting format, and download the file. Import it into SAGA or WinMENTOR using that software's standard import wizard.

### WinMENTOR Enterprise (REST API)

WinMENTOR Enterprise supports direct API integration.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the WinMENTOR Enterprise card">
    In **Settings → Integrations**, click **WinMENTOR Enterprise**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the REST endpoint">
    Enter the REST API endpoint URL provided by your WinMENTOR Enterprise administrator. It typically looks like `https://yourcompany.winmentor.ro/api/`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter credentials">
    Enter the API username and password configured in WinMENTOR Enterprise.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test and save">
    Click **Test Connection**, then **Save**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## 5. SmartBill

SmartBill is a Romanian cloud invoicing platform. With this integration active, Druma pushes confirmed invoices to SmartBill automatically — no manual re-entry.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Get your SmartBill API key">
    In your SmartBill account, go to **Cont → API** and copy your API key.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the SmartBill card in Druma">
    Go to **Settings → Integrations → SmartBill**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your API key and CIF">
    Paste the API key and enter your company's CIF (fiscal registration number). SmartBill uses the CIF to route invoices to the correct company account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test the connection">
    Click **Test Connection**. Druma will attempt a handshake with SmartBill and confirm the credentials are valid.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. From this point, every invoice you mark as confirmed in Druma will be pushed to SmartBill automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
If your SmartBill subscription lapses or your API key is regenerated, the integration will stop working silently. Check **Settings → Integrations** periodically to confirm the SmartBill status is green.
</Warning>

## 6. Peppol E-Invoicing (Belgium)

Peppol is the pan-European e-invoicing network. Belgian companies (KBO-registered) are required to send invoices to other Belgian businesses via Peppol — this has been mandatory since January 2026.

**Required for:** Belgian companies sending B2B invoices to other Belgian businesses.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the Peppol E-Invoicing card">
    Go to **Settings → Integrations** and click the **Peppol E-Invoicing** card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your registration details">
    Enter your **KBO number** (Belgian company registration number) and your **VAT number**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Register on the Peppol Network">
    Click **Register on Peppol Network**. Druma submits your registration to the e-invoice.be access point. This typically completes within a few minutes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm activation">
    Once active, the card shows a green **Active** status. Peppol routing is now automatic for eligible Belgian clients — no extra steps are needed when sending invoices to Belgian recipients.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
See [Peppol E-Invoicing](/en/integrations/peppol-invoicing) for full details on how the routing works, what happens when a recipient is not on the Peppol network, and how to handle rejections.
</Note>

## 7. Supplier Invoice Email Inbox

This gives your company a dedicated email address for receiving supplier invoices — from fuel card providers, workshop services, toll operators, or any other supplier. Received invoices are AI-classified and routed to a review queue, so nothing slips through.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Company Settings">
    Navigate to **Settings → Company** and scroll to the **Supplier Invoice Email** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable the inbox">
    Toggle on **Enable supplier invoice inbox**. Druma generates a dedicated inbound email address for your company.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add sender addresses to the whitelist">
    In the **Sender Whitelist** field, add the email addresses or domains you expect invoices from (for example, `invoices@dkv.com` or `@uta.eu`). Emails from non-whitelisted senders are rejected and never enter your queue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share the address with your suppliers">
    Copy the generated email address and give it to each supplier as their invoice-submission address for your company.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Received invoices appear in **Finance → Forwarding → Invoice Queue** for review, matching, and approval.

<Note>
See [Supplier Invoices](/en/invoicing/supplier-invoices) for full details on the review queue, auto-matching logic, and how to handle unmatched invoices.
</Note>
