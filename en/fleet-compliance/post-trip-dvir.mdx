## Overview

The Post-Trip Inspection — also called a Driver Vehicle Inspection Report (DVIR) — is a structured safety check that drivers complete at the end of each trip. It identifies defects before the truck goes back into service.

Druma's DVIR system works on the mobile driver app. Defects are categorised by severity. Safety-critical defects trigger an automatic block on the vehicle and notify the fleet manager immediately.

---

## How Drivers Complete a DVIR

The DVIR is initiated from the driver app when an order reaches **Offloading Complete** or **Delivered** status:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the inspection prompt">
    Tap the **Post-Trip Inspection** prompt that appears at the bottom of the completed order in the driver app.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Work through the checklist">
    A structured form opens covering the vehicle's key systems. For each item, select: **OK**, **Defect — Minor**, or **Defect — Safety Critical**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Report any defects">
    For any defect, enter a description and optionally attach a photo (up to 5 photos per defect item).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign the inspection">
    At the end of the checklist, sign with a digital signature on-screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Tap **Submit Inspection**. The report is sent to Druma immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Inspection Checklist Categories

| Category | Items covered |
|---|---|
| **Exterior** | Bodywork damage, lights (head, tail, indicator, brake), reflectors, mirrors |
| **Tyres and wheels** | Tread depth, visible damage, wheel nuts secure |
| **Brakes** | Air pressure, visible brake component damage |
| **Engine compartment** | Fluid levels (oil, coolant, windscreen wash), visible leaks |
| **Cab interior** | Dashboard warning lights, horn, wipers, seat belts |
| **Trailer coupling** | Kingpin, landing legs, electrical connector, airlines |
| **Cargo area** | Floor condition, lashing points, side curtains/panels |
| **Documentation** | CMR documents, transport licence, tachograph discs |

---

## Severity Levels

| Level | What it means | Druma action |
|---|---|---|
| **OK** | No defect — item is in acceptable condition | No action |
| **Minor defect** | Defect noted but vehicle can remain in service | Amber badge on vehicle; fleet manager notified by email |
| **Safety Critical** | Defect that makes the vehicle unsafe to operate | Red badge on vehicle; vehicle blocked from new order assignments; fleet manager notified immediately |

<Warning>
A vehicle with a Safety Critical defect is automatically blocked from assignment on the planning board until a fleet manager explicitly clears the block. This cannot be overridden by dispatchers — only a Fleet Manager, Admin, or Company Admin can clear the block by marking the related work order as resolved.
</Warning>

## Viewing DVIR Results (Planner / Fleet Manager)

**Fleet → Defects** shows all open defects across the fleet:

- One row per defect (not per inspection)
- Severity badge (Minor / Safety Critical)
- Vehicle plate and driver name
- Defect description and attached photos
- Date and time of the inspection
- **Create Work Order** button (Safety Critical defects only)

Click any row to expand the full inspection report — including all items checked, the driver signature, and the linked order.

---

## Creating a Work Order from a Defect

When a Safety Critical defect appears in **Fleet → Defects**, click **Create Work Order** on that row. The work order form opens pre-filled with:

- The truck that was inspected
- The defect item name and description as the work order description
- Type set to **Safety Critical**

Add line items and a scheduled date, then save. The work order blocks the truck on the planning board until it is marked as complete.

See [Workshop & Maintenance](/en/fleet-compliance/workshop-maintenance) for the full work order workflow.

---

## Making DVIR Mandatory

DVIR is **not automatically mandatory** — the driver is prompted but can skip it by default.

**To make DVIR mandatory after delivery:**

Go to **Settings → Company → Fleet → Require post-trip inspection after delivery** → enable the toggle.

When this is on, the driver cannot mark an order as **Delivered Complete** until the DVIR checklist is submitted.

<Note>
DVIR is recorded per order, not per calendar day. If a driver completes two deliveries in one day, they will be prompted to complete a DVIR at the end of each. This may be adjusted to once-per-day in a future release.
</Note>
